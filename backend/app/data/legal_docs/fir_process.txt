FIR (First Information Report) Process

An FIR is the first official document recorded by police when a cognizable offense is reported.

Steps in Filing an FIR:
1. Visit the nearest police station.
2. Provide complete details of the incident.
3. Police record the statement.
4. FIR number is generated.
5. Investigation begins.

Important Points:
- FIR must be registered for cognizable offenses.
- Citizen has the right to receive a copy of the FIR free of cost.
- FIR can be filed by the victim or any witness.

DATA FIELDS IN IF1:
- Field 1: District, Police Station (P.S.), Year, FIR Number, and Date.
- Field 2: List of Acts and specific Sections violated.
- Field 3a: Occurrence of Offense (Day, Date, and Time range).
- Field 3b: Time when information was officially received at the P.S.
- Field 5: Place of Occurrence including address and distance/direction from the P.S.
- Field 6: Full details of the Complainant/Informant (Name, Father's Name, Nationality).

If Police Refuse to Register FIR:
- Approach the Superintendent of Police under Section 154(3) CrPC.
- Send complaint via registered post.
- Approach Magistrate under Section 156(3) CrPC.

Online FIR facilities are available in many states.
