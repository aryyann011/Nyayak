Domestic Violence Legal Protection

Domestic violence includes physical, emotional, verbal, economic, and sexual abuse within a household.

Protection Under Law:
- Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005.

Victim Rights:
1. Right to protection order.
2. Right to residence order.
3. Right to monetary relief.
4. Right to custody of children.
5. Right to compensation.

Steps to Take:
- Contact nearest police station.
- Approach Protection Officer.
- File complaint in Magistrate court.
- Seek NGO or legal aid support.

Emergency helpline numbers are available in most states.

Domestic violence cases are treated seriously and courts may provide urgent protection.
